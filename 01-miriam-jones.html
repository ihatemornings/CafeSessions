Café Sessions : 1. Miriam Jones

1. Miriam Jones

Miriam Jones is a Canadian songwriter living in Oxford and I reckon she’s amazing, so I asked her to be my first guest on the Café Sessions. She played us two songs and we chatted about songwriting, capos, the Oxford scene and the warming qualities of different sock types.

After a couple of years in Oxford, Miriam has exhausted the possibilities of the Jericho Tavern. Now she plays most of her gigs in London, and puts lots of stuff on the internet. Last year she released a series of monthly recordings, the Solitary Songs, which are stunning.

We talked about how it's difficult to even get a gig if you can't be loud or bring fifty drunken students through the door. But then we cheered up a bit, brought out the ukulele and finished the session with a cheeky version of Miriam's song Fancy Free.

Café Sessions

Songwriters live and acoustic in Oxford

Oxford is a great city for music, with hundreds of bands and a vibrant indie scene. But there's never been a culture of café gigs, where local songwriters can try their luck with a well-behaved cake-munching crowd.

Rather than getting all melancholy about the difficult lot of the acoustic singer/songwriter, I'm inviting Oxford's finest up to my flat to record video sessions. They play a couple of songs and we chat. I live stream the whole session, then edit the recording down and post it here.

If you're interested in playing or you want to recommend somebody, email me or tell me on Twitter. ;)

— Ben Walker
